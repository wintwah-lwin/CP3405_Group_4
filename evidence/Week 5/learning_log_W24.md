# Learning Log – Week 24

## What We Believed

The team expected markets to move higher because:

- All three major indices remained above their weekly 8 EMA and 21 EMA trend lines, preserving bullish intermediate-term structure.
- Falling Treasury yields, weakening crude oil prices, and a softer US dollar provided a favorable macro backdrop for risk assets.
- Strong Russell 2000 participation during the previous week suggested market breadth was expanding beyond mega-cap technology stocks.

## What Happened

The bullish thesis largely played out as expected.

Results:

- SPX: +0.78%
- NDX: +2.31%
- IWM: +0.97%

Technology continued to lead the market as semiconductor companies maintained strong momentum. The Nasdaq 100 significantly outperformed broader indices, while the S&P 500 and Russell 2000 posted more moderate gains. Energy markets weakened sharply as WTI crude oil fell nearly 9% for the week, helping ease inflation concerns and supporting equity valuations.

## What We Learned

1. Trend Structure Matters

When all three major indices remain above rising weekly moving averages, trend-following signals deserve significant weight. Strong technical structures often survive short-term news volatility.

2. Breadth Improves Rally Durability

The Russell 2000's positive performance confirmed that market participation remains relatively broad. Healthy breadth reduces dependence on a handful of mega-cap stocks.

3. Falling Oil Can Be a Bullish Catalyst

The sharp decline in crude oil prices acted as a tailwind for equities by reducing inflation pressure and improving expectations for future monetary policy flexibility.

## What We Will Change Next Week

- Continue monitoring sector leadership within Technology, especially semiconductor stocks versus software stocks.
- Track whether small-cap participation remains healthy or begins to weaken.
- Place greater emphasis on commodity movements, particularly crude oil, as a leading indicator for inflation expectations and risk sentiment.
