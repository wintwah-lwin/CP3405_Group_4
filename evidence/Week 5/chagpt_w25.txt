Weekly Market Regime Recommendation — Week of 22 June 2026

MARKET REGIME: Neutral-Bullish with High Volatility Risk

CONFIDENCE: Medium

FINAL WEEKLY THESIS: The market enters the week with mixed signals.
Technical conditions remain positive because the S&P 500, Nasdaq 100,
and Russell 2000 are all trading above their 8 EMA and 21 EMA, showing
that the overall weekly trend remains bullish. Nasdaq 100 is the
strongest index, supported by strong semiconductor performance and
AI-related demand.

However, seasonal patterns create downside risks. The week after June
Triple Witching has historically been weak, especially for the Dow
Jones. This suggests possible short-term volatility or a pullback during
the early part of the week.

Macro conditions are slightly supportive. Treasury yields declined, oil
prices dropped sharply, and inflation pressure may be easing. However,
the Federal Reserve remains cautious, with higher expectations of future
rate increases creating uncertainty for growth stocks.

The main event risk is the Core PCE inflation report on 25 June. A
softer inflation result could support equities, while a
higher-than-expected result may increase rate concerns. Geopolitical
risks from Middle East tensions also remain a potential source of
volatility.

OVERALL MARKET VIEW: The recommended market regime is Neutral-Bullish.
The technical trend is positive, but seasonal weakness and macro
uncertainty prevent a stronger bullish view.
