# Learning Log – Week 23

## What We Believed

The team believed that the market would remain neutral to slightly positive because:

- Technical trends remained bullish.
- Technology earnings were strong.
- The Federal Reserve is expected to keep interest rates unchanged.

However, we also recognised risks from:

- Rising oil prices
- Geopolitical tensions
- Weak midterm-year seasonality

## What Happened

The market experienced a significantly larger decline than expected.

Results:

- SPX: -2.57%
- NDX: -4.53%
- IWM: -3.06%

Technology stocks failed to provide support and became one of the weakest areas of the market.

## What We Learned

1. Strong technical trends may fail when macro risks increase rapidly.

2. Multiple AI models reached very similar conclusions, possibly because they used the same calculation model, which made them into consensus bias.

3. As human score challenge the AI consensus and place greater emphasis on geopolitical and oil-related risks, speculation gains more reference value.

## What We Will Change Next Week

- Increase weighting on macro and geopolitical factors.
- Pay more attention to market breadth.
- Avoid relying too heavily on technology leadership.
- Before finalizing the prediction, proactively offer suggestions that differ from those consensus of AI.
